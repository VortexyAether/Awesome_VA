## New Papers / Tools

### PA-SciML — physics-audited agentic surrogate discovery

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07379
- Type: Paper / agentic SciML validation workflow
- Why it matters: Error-only agentic model search can pick plausible surrogates that violate boundary conditions, stiffness scaling, superposition, or causality. PA-SciML fixes the evaluator before search, derives reviewable machine-checkable physics requirements, audits trained candidate outputs, and searches for high-violation inputs even without reference solution fields.
- Evidence: arXiv abstract reports computational solid-mechanics examples where selected surrogates are only reported as verified under stated checks, separating validation-error ranking from physics-violation evidence.
- Caveat: Evidence is solid-mechanics-focused; CFD/turbulence transfer needs conservation, spectral-error, boundary-family shift, and rollout checks defined separately. Code/log release was not confirmed.
- Action: Read/Cite

### ArtisanCAD — CAD-IR + CATIA-MCP for editable industrial CAD procedures

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.05750
- Type: Paper / CAD agent architecture
- Why it matters: Industrial CAD agents need long-horizon procedural modeling, robust feature dependencies, editable parametric geometry, and production-grade B-Rep execution. ArtisanCAD’s CAD-IR encodes parameters, ordered operations, MCP tool bindings, dependencies, generated entities, and verification rules, then distills expert CATIA procedures into reusable skills.
- Evidence: arXiv API abstract explicitly frames CAD-IR as an executable procedural scaffold for ambiguous or underspecified prompts; the Issue Board scan recorded a Text2CAD Chamfer Distance claim of 14.83 → 9.88.
- Caveat: CATIA recordings and industrial workflow assumptions may not transfer directly to open FreeCAD/CadQuery stacks; editability and feature robustness need independent checks.
- Action: Cite/Watch

### BubbleSH — deformable bubble-swarm DNS dataset

- Link: https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.07275
- Dataset: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.21229301
- Type: Dataset / multiphase-flow benchmark
- Why it matters: CFD surrogate benchmarks need chaotic interaction and shape dynamics, not only smooth field RMSE. BubbleSH provides transient 3D bubble-swarm DNS with trajectories, velocities, and spherical-harmonic shape evolution, plus metrics for trajectory and shape prediction.
- Evidence: arXiv abstract describes high-fidelity DNS in a periodic rising-bubble domain; Zenodo lists `Data.zip` at 9.2 GB and `README.md` at 4.4 kB under CC-BY-4.0.
- Caveat: Bubbly flow is specialized; schema, splits, and baseline scripts still need inspection before using it as a local benchmark.
- Action: Save/Cite

## Best Find of the Day

**PA-SciML.** It makes the strongest generalizable point for VA’s Engineering AI positioning: a surrogate is not “trusted” because the leaderboard score is low; it is trusted only under explicit physics checks, stated tolerances, and searchable violation evidence.

## Engineering-AI Pattern

**Turn claims into audit packets.** For CFD/CAD/CAE agents, the useful artifact is not just a predicted field or generated part. It is the field/part plus boundary-condition checks, geometry/procedure metadata, violation search, fallback criteria, and enough provenance to rerun the decision.
